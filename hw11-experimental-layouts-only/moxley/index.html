Mutual Aid in the New River Valley

In organization theory, mutual aid is a voluntary reciprocal exchange of resources and services for mutual benefit. Mutual aid projects are a form of political participation in which people take responsibility for caring for one another and changing political conditions. -- Wikipedia

This site hopes to serve as a repository for all kinds of mutual aid projects in and around the New River Valley. From Little Free Libraries to organizations like Food Not Bombs, no project is too small or to large to be included. Find something that interests you and get involved today!

Looking Out For Each Other: New River Valley

"Looking Out for Each Other formed as a Facebook group. The coordinating team started in order to guide that group and gather resources to help the New River Valley."

This site and its associated Facebook group serves as almost a home page for other MA/DA groups and activities in the area. Not every active project is listed, but this is a great place to start.

Southwest Virginia Virtual Tip Jar

The SWVA Virtual Tip Jar is a way for employees hit by the pandemic shutdowns be listed online so that patrons can send them a tip through their cash apps. The SWVA Virtual Tip Jar is modeled after Philadelphia's program which has been very successful.
